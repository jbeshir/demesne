Audit whatever interaction data a founder has actually collected, design the feedback loop that turns ongoing usage into systematic product improvement, and draft a one-page moat narrative that survives adversarial review. You author one orchestrator prompt, launch a single slow-tier `sandbox_agent`, and it runs the pipeline autonomously. The deliverable is `/out/moat-narrative.md` plus the flywheel design and data inventory ‚Äî or, when the data is thin, an honest verdict and a collection plan instead. Report-only; no code landing.

**Watch out (cross-cutting):** The failure mode this skill exists to prevent is a moat narrative backfilled from vibes ‚Äî a flywheel story written about data that is too thin, too short, or too disconnected to support it. Every quantitative claim (how long the flywheel has spun, how many users it covers) must come from the deterministic profiling step, never from the founder's recollection or an agent's estimate; and the narrative ships only after the adversary child has tried to kill it. Foundational: the orchestrator copies deliverables into its own `/out` itself ‚Äî a `sandbox_script` child's `/out` is `/out/child/<name>/` and delegating the final copy strands the files.

## Procedure

1. **INTAKE + PROFILE.** The orchestrator lists everything under `/in/data` and writes `/workspace/manifest.jsonl` (`{"path", "size_bytes", "type"}` per file). Then a `sandbox_script` child (`name=profile-data`, `image=python`, `egress=package-managers`) computes, per file: record count, timestamp span (earliest/latest), event types present, distinct-user count, and whether a stable user identifier joins records across files and time. Results to `/workspace/profile/stats.json`; files it cannot parse are logged with reason to `/workspace/profile/unparsed.md`, never silently skipped ‚Äî founders export messy CSVs, half-broken JSON, and raw DB dumps, and a skipped file understates the moat. Profiling is deterministic work: a script measures it, an LLM does not estimate it.

2. **DATA VERDICT.** In the orchestrator's own process, apply the thresholds (embedded in the prompt, step 3 below) to `stats.json` and write `/workspace/verdict.md`: **FLYWHEEL-READY**, **PARTIAL**, or **THIN-DATA**, with the measured numbers that produced it. On THIN-DATA, skip steps 3, 5, and 6: run step 4 in design-forward mode (what loop the product *should* have and what to instrument to feed it) and deliver inventory + verdict + collection plan. A thin-data verdict with a collection plan is a valid, useful outcome ‚Äî the pipeline must not manufacture a narrative to avoid it.

3. **MINE ‚Äî fan out pattern miners.** Shard the parseable corpus by context budget (event logs: thousands of records/shard; analytics exports ~20‚Äì50 files; the profiling stats tell you sizes) into `/workspace/shard-NN.jsonl` path lists. Spawn one medium-tier `sandbox_agent` per shard (`name=miner-01`, `miner-02`, ‚Ä¶) with `background: true`, collect `job_id`s, poll `sandbox_wait`, keep **‚â§8 in flight** (rolling window beyond that). Blocking calls are issued one per turn and run strictly sequentially ‚Äî background dispatch is the only way miners run concurrently. Each miner's prompt embeds its exact path list and the locked schema, and it writes `/out/patterns.jsonl` (one record per candidate pattern: `pattern_id`, `description`, `evidence` (source paths + record references), `users_covered`, `time_span`, `signal_type` (longitudinal vs cross-sectional), `why_high_signal`) plus `/out/log.md` for anything skipped. Lock the schema before the first miner launches; divergent shards cannot be merged downstream.

4. **DESIGN THE LOOP.** Only after every miner reaches a terminal state (`sandbox_wait` on all `job_id`s ‚Äî this barrier holds; sibling files appear at `/in/previous-jobs/miner-NN/` only on completion), spawn one slow-tier `sandbox_agent` (`name=loop-designer`). It ranks the mined patterns, keeps the 3‚Äì5 highest-signal, and for each designs the full loop: **signal** (the named events/fields, traced to the manifest) ‚Üí **aggregation** (how and how often it's computed) ‚Üí **decision point** (who or what consumes it, and the threshold that triggers action) ‚Üí **product change** (the concrete mechanism: ranking weights, defaults, prompts, roadmap input) ‚Üí **measurement** (the metric that confirms the change worked), plus cycle time. A loop citing data that does not exist in the manifest is fiction ‚Äî every signal traces to a profiled field. Writes `/out/flywheel.md`; on PARTIAL or THIN-DATA also `/out/collection-plan.md` (instrumentation spec for the signals not yet captured: event, properties, identifier requirements, retention, and the date at which re-running this audit becomes worthwhile).

5. **DRAFT THE NARRATIVE.** Spawn a slow-tier `sandbox_agent` (`name=narrator`) reading `/in/previous-jobs/loop-designer/flywheel.md` and `/workspace/profile/stats.json`. It drafts `/out/moat-narrative.md` ‚Äî **one page, hard cap**: how the flywheel works, how long it has been spinning (the measured earliest-continuous-coverage date, not the founding date), and why a well-resourced competitor starting today cannot replicate it in under two years. Every claim carries an inline evidence tag pointing at a pattern_id, a stat, or a designed loop.

6. **PRESSURE-TEST.** Spawn a fresh slow-tier `sandbox_agent` (`name=adversary`) ‚Äî never the narrator scoring its own work. Its role: strategy lead at a well-funded competitor, tasked with replicating this position in under two years, budget not a constraint. It attacks each narrative claim through three lenses: (a) **replicable-by-purchase** ‚Äî could the data be bought, scraped, or synthesized; (b) **generic-insight** ‚Äî would a competent PM guess this pattern without any data; (c) **unwired-loop** ‚Äî is there evidence usage ever actually changed the product (deploy history or changelog in the mount is the strongest rebuttal), or is the loop aspirational. Where a claim hinges on data uniqueness, the adversary may request one `sandbox_research` child (`name=adversary-recon`, open egress) to check public availability ‚Äî it gets a fresh private workspace and **no `/in` mounts**, so the claims under test are embedded verbatim in its prompt and its findings harvested from `/out/child/adversary-recon/`. Per-claim verdicts: HOLDS / CUT / CONTESTED, written to `/out/attack.md`. Claims that fail without counter-evidence are cut from the narrative, not softened. Revision loop: `narrator-r2` revises, `adversary-r2` re-attacks ‚Äî **cap 2 rounds** (fresh names each round; reusing a child name errors and poisons subsequent sibling mounts); anything still disputed lands in the narrative's Contested Claims footer rather than being silently kept or dropped.

7. **DELIVER.** In the orchestrator's own process, `cp` the final artefacts from `/out/child/<name>/` into the orchestrator's own `/out/` per the contract below, and write `/out/SUMMARY.md` (verdict, files profiled/unparsed, patterns mined, claims held/cut/contested, children spawned).

## Writing the orchestrator prompt

Brief it as a complete document:

1. **Product context** ‚Äî what the product does, the vertical, when it went live, and what the founder *believes* the data advantage is. The belief is an input to be tested, not a conclusion to be decorated.
2. **The mount** ‚Äî data lives at `/in/data`; profiling is deterministic-first; unparseable files logged, never skipped.
3. **Thin-data thresholds, verbatim** ‚Äî issue THIN-DATA if any of: total timestamp span < 90 days; no stable user identifier linking a user's records over time (engagement-over-time is unobservable without one); fewer than 50 users with activity in 2+ distinct weeks. PARTIAL if thresholds pass but coverage gaps mean some claimed loops can't be evidenced. State plainly: THIN-DATA with a collection plan is a valid outcome, not a failure.
4. **The mining schema, locked verbatim** ‚Äî the seven fields from step 3, finalised before any miner launches.
5. **The loop schema, verbatim** ‚Äî signal ‚Üí aggregation ‚Üí decision point ‚Üí product change ‚Üí measurement, plus cycle time; 3‚Äì5 loops; every signal traces to a manifest field.
6. **Narrative spec** ‚Äî one page hard cap; three sections (The Flywheel, Spin History, Replication Barrier); spin date measured, every claim evidence-tagged.
7. **Adversary brief** ‚Äî the competitor-strategy-lead role, the three attack lenses, the cut-don't-soften rule, the optional recon child with claims embedded in its prompt, the 2-round cap, and the Contested Claims footer.
8. **Fan-out mechanics** ‚Äî background dispatch + `sandbox_wait`, ‚â§8 in flight, the miner barrier before loop-designer, and that the orchestrator performs the final copy itself.
9. **Output contract** ‚Äî the tree below; report-only, no edits or commits.

## Output contract

```
/out/
  moat-narrative.md    # the one-page deliverable, post-adversary (absent on THIN-DATA)
  flywheel.md          # 3‚Äì5 loop designs: signal‚Üíaggregation‚Üídecision‚Üíchange‚Üímeasurement
  inventory.md         # what's collected, since when, coverage ‚Äî measured numbers
  data-verdict.md      # FLYWHEEL-READY | PARTIAL | THIN-DATA, with the numbers behind it
  collection-plan.md   # present on PARTIAL and THIN-DATA
  patterns.jsonl       # mined behavioural patterns (absent on THIN-DATA)
  attack.md            # claim-by-claim adversary verdicts, including what was cut and why
  SUMMARY.md           # run summary: verdict, files profiled/unparsed, claims held/cut, children
```

`moat-narrative.md` sections in order: **The Flywheel** (how usage becomes improvement, naming the designed loops), **Spin History** (measured start date, users and records accumulated), **Replication Barrier** (the under-two-years case, claim by claim), **Contested Claims** (footer; present only if round-2 disputes remain). One page total ‚Äî a narrative that needs two pages is hiding a weak claim in volume.

## Launching the orchestrator

- **`directories: ["<abs path to the founder's data exports>"]` is mandatory** ‚Äî mounted at `/in/data`. Forgetting it gives the profiler nothing to measure and the run degenerates to an evidence-free THIN-DATA verdict. The founder should put in whatever exists: product-analytics exports (Mixpanel/Amplitude/PostHog CSV or JSON), raw event logs, database dumps of usage tables, support or feedback exports, and ‚Äî valuable for the unwired-loop defence ‚Äî deploy history or a changelog showing usage data has driven past product changes. Formats are flexible; children must handle messy real-world files and log the unparseable ones.
- Tiers: **slow** for the orchestrator, loop-designer, narrator, and adversary; **medium** for miners; `sandbox_script` for profiling.
- Child names (lowercase letters, digits, interior hyphens, ‚â§40 chars): `profile-data`, `miner-01`‚Ä¶, `loop-designer`, `narrator`, `adversary`, `adversary-recon`, `narrator-r2`, `adversary-r2`.
